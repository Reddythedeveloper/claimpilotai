# Prior Authorization Policy - 2026 Q1
**Policy ID:** policy_102_4
**Category:** authorization_rule

## General Requirements
All inpatient admissions and high-dollar outpatient procedures (exceeding $5,000) require prior authorization from the health plan before services are rendered.
If an authorization is missing at the time of claim submission, the claim will be denied with reason code "Missing authorization".

## Exceptions
Emergency services (e.g., procedure code 99285) do not require prior authorization if the patient is admitted through the emergency department and the diagnosis is a life-threatening condition (e.g., J18.9 - Pneumonia, unspecified organism with respiratory distress). In these cases, retro-authorization must be obtained within 48 hours of admission.

## Resubmission
To overturn a "Missing authorization" denial, providers must submit the clinical encounter notes clearly demonstrating the emergency nature of the visit or attach the retro-authorization approval letter.
